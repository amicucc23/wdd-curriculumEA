Good and Bad Designs in the Real World

Interaction design has become a hot commodity. More and more companies are turning to interactive designers to help them discover ways to improve the products they create. By thinking of all the various ways users will interact with these products, designers can work to improve the usability aspects, thus increasing the potential that people will want to continuing using it. In other words, if it's hard to figure out or use, people won't use it. THIS ACTIVITY IS DUE AT THE BEGINNING OF LECTURE 2.

The purpose of this exercise is to get you thinking about everyday artifacts that we all interact with in the real world. Don't think computers or phones, think analog or tangible. What makes these things work? More importantly, what makes them work so well?
