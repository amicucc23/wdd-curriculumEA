Homepage Wireframe

When developing a solution for any clients Ui you should adhere to any functional specifications required for the Ui, as they are outlined in the functional specifications document. Wireframes are a great way to represent all aspects of a Ui with a lot less development time. They are meant to be a rough outline of the final Ui. Once the client has approved the wireframe, you can then precede to the visual design of the Ui. These designs are commonly referred to as “design comps” or “screens” (short for screen shots) and should reflect the final look and feel of the entire Ui. In other words design comps should look exactly like the finished product, as it will exist online.

What is a functional spec? The main goal of a functional specifications document is to outline the functionality and criteria required for the website or application and is usually drafted during the Concept Phase of the project. It is meant to act as a blueprint that can be referred to by the client and developer during the course of the project to ensure all project requirements have been met. Once the document has been reviewed and signed off by both parties, the Design Phase can begin.
